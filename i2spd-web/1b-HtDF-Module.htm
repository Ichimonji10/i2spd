How to Design Functions Module Help Learn more.

This module is where this course starts to differ from other introductory programming courses and this should be new material for everyone - we are going to start to learn the design method. We repeat the advice that this is material you learn by doing: type along with the video, do the practice problems, do the homework problems.

The material in this module should take approximately 4-5 hours of dedicated time to complete, including working along with the lecture videos, doing the practice problems, doing the homework problems and the short quiz.

NOTE: This course has more video minutes than some other courses, but that does not mean that it has more material. Because this course teaches a design method, much of the video material consists of the instructor working through a complete problem step-by-step. As a result, in most of the videos at least part of the material is essentially review, with new points interspersed. The alternative would be to have videos that jump right into the middle of a problem to address only new material. But our prior students have told us that these videos are much more useful since they show complete solutions and clearly show how new material integrates with old material.

Learning Goals

  • Be able to use the How to Design Functions ( HtDF) recipe to design functions that operate on primitive data.

The next 4 video lectures are short examples of using the the HtDF recipe. Each video is partly review of the HtDF recipe, but each video also elaborates on how to use the recipe, including points on:

  • writing signatures and purpose statements
  • how many tests a function should have
  • what to do when tests fail
  • what to do when your understanding of the purpose changes part way through the design

We recommend that you work through the problems along with the video. Get the starter file, work one step of the recipe, watch that on video, then work the next step of the recipe, watch that and so on. Remember, you can't learn program design by watching -- you have to do it!

Tips for Success

General tips for doing well in this course can be found on the To Do Well in This Course page. But here are a few suggestions specific to this module:

  • At first the HtDF recipe may either seem like overkill, or overwhelming. You may suspect that these functions could be designed more quickly without the recipe. You are right! But please take the time to learn the recipe now. It will pay you back later by making really hard problems much easier to solve.
  • If at any point in time you don't know what to do, ask yourself: What step of the recipe am I at? What am I supposed to be writing now? And where am I supposed to look to get help with that?
  • If the above doesn't work, and you get stuck on a problem for more than 5 minutes, try to find a similar problem and see what happened at the corresponding point in that design.
  • Also take advantage of the discussion forums to ask questions. There are many thousands of students in this course. If you post a question on the Discussion Forums you will often be able to get help from another student. In addition, posting there will enable the course staff (instructor and TAs) to help you.
